Wholesale toy assortment planning

Buy for a channel,
not for a trend headline.

Popular products can fail when the package, price architecture, carton quantity or merchandising story does not fit the destination channel. This field note offers a repeatable way to compare categories before requesting samples.

Decision framework

Four questions before “Is it hot?”

A

Where will it sell?

Marketplace, specialty retail, value channel, party supply, seasonal kiosk and promotion programs need different price, pack and content structures.

B

Why will it be picked up?

Define the visible play value: transformation, motion, sensory feedback, collectability, outdoor action, gifting or educational interaction.

C

How will it travel?

Compare unit size, retail package, carton quantity, CBM, gross weight and damage risk before a low unit price becomes expensive logistics.

D

What must be proved?

Age grading, warnings, material declarations, battery documentation and destination-market evidence should be considered at shortlist stage.

Category desk

Three product families, three buying logics

CATEGORY 01

Summer action toys

Water guns and bubble products are easy to understand at first glance, but product range, refill method, battery specification, liquid handling and seasonal timing strongly affect the final offer.

Useful for
Pool, beach, outdoor retail, summer campaigns
Compare
Capacity, range, mechanism, carton CBM, display impact
Ask first
Destination market, launch date and pack format

CATEGORY 02

Squishy & stress-relief toys

Sensory feedback creates strong demonstration value, but buyers should compare material feel, odor, recovery behavior, surface finish, small components, individual packing and display quantity.

Useful for
Impulse, novelty, gifting, rewards and sensory assortments
Compare
Texture, rebound, piece size, display box and assortment mix
Ask first
Material evidence, samples and approved color range
Review current hot-selling product facts

CATEGORY 03

Educational & activity toys

Visible learning claims are less important than the actual interaction. Buyers should document age fit, repeat play, instructions, included pieces, assembly, small-parts considerations and shelf communication.

Useful for
Gift, parent-child, preschool and school-reward channels
Compare
Interaction, piece count, instructions, package storytelling
Ask first
Age grade, sample test and approved claim wording
